Layer 1 — Global Masters

Foundation records that every other module depends on. Set these up first before any transactions.

Global Master

Company

The top-level legal entity. Defines the default currency, country, chart of accounts, and fiscal year. Every transaction, employee, and asset belongs to a Company.

→ Department→ Cost Center→ Account→ Fiscal Year
Global Master

Department

Organisational unit within a Company (e.g. Engineering, Finance). Used to group employees, filter payroll runs, and tag GL entries as a cost dimension.

→ Employee→ Payroll Entry (filter)→ Asset (dimension)→ Journal Entry (dimension)
Global Master

Cost Center

Accounting dimension for tracking where money is spent. Linked to payroll, expense claims, assets, and journal entries to enable profit-center reporting.

→ Payroll Entry→ Salary Slip→ Expense Claim→ Asset→ Journal Entry
Global Master

Account

A single node in the Chart of Accounts (asset, liability, income, expense, equity). Every financial posting references an Account. Parent of GL Entry rows.

→ Journal Entry (accounts table)
Global Master

Currency

Defines exchange rates and currency symbols. Referenced by Income Tax Slabs and Salary Structures to ensure correct monetary calculations across multi-currency setups.

→ Income Tax Slab→ Salary Structure
Global Master

Finance Book

Allows parallel depreciation books (e.g. GAAP vs Tax). Asset Categories and Assets reference a Finance Book to post depreciation to the correct ledger.

→ Asset Category→ Asset
Global Master

Fiscal Year

Defines the accounting year start/end dates. Controls which period transactions fall into and drives year-end closing entries.

← Company
Global Master

Holiday List

A named list of non-working days (public holidays, company holidays). Assigned to Shift Types and Leave Types to correctly calculate working days and leave balances.

→ Shift Type→ Leave Type
Layer 2 — People Masters

Core records for users, customers, suppliers, and items — the "who" and "what" of the system.

People Master

User

A login account in the system. Linked to an Employee record via user_id. Drives role-based access, leave approvals, expense approvals, interview scheduling, and CRM ownership.

→ Role→ User Permission→ Employee→ CRM Lead (owner)→ CRM Deal (owner)→ Leave Application (approver)→ Expense Claim (approver)→ Interview (interviewer)→ Project (user)→ CRM Task (assigned_to)
People Master

Role

A named permission profile (e.g. "HR Manager", "Accounts User"). Users are assigned one or more Roles which control which doctypes and fields they can read/write/submit.

← User
People Master

User Permission

Row-level security rules. Restricts a User to only see records belonging to specific Companies, Cost Centers, or other master values. Enforced automatically on all list views and reports.

← User
People Master

Customer

An external party that buys goods or services. Linked to Projects (for client billing) and Sales Invoices. Can be synced from a converted CRM Deal.

→ Project (customer)→ Sales Invoice (billed to)← CRM Deal (sync)
People Master

Supplier

An external party that supplies goods or services. Can be recorded as the owner of an Asset (e.g. leased equipment).

→ Asset (asset_owner)
People Master

Contact

An individual person associated with a Customer, Supplier, or CRM record. Stores phone, email, and address. Linked into CRM Deal's contacts table for relationship tracking.

→ CRM Deal (contacts table)
People Master

Item

A product or service SKU. When flagged as "Is Fixed Asset", it becomes the source item for creating an Asset record upon purchase receipt.

→ Asset (item_code, Is Fixed Asset)
Frappe CRM

Pipeline management from lead capture through deal closure. Runs as a separate Vue SPA at /frappe-crm.

CRM Setup — Configuration masters that govern all CRM behaviour
CRM Setup

Lead Status

Configurable status values for the Lead pipeline (e.g. New, Contacted, Qualified). Drives the Kanban column labels on the Leads board.

→ CRM Lead (status)
CRM Setup

Deal Status

Configurable status values for the Deal pipeline (e.g. Proposal Sent, Negotiation, Won). Drives the Kanban column labels on the Deals board.

→ CRM Deal (status)
CRM Setup

Lead Source

Where a lead originated (e.g. Website, Referral, Cold Call). Used for source attribution reporting on both Leads and Deals.

→ CRM Lead (source)→ CRM Deal (source)
CRM Setup

Industry

Industry vertical of the prospect (e.g. Manufacturing, Healthcare). Enables segmentation and filtering of leads and deals by vertical.

→ CRM Lead→ CRM Deal
CRM Setup

Territory

Geographic or sales territory (e.g. North India, APAC). Used to assign leads and deals to regional sales teams and for territory-based reporting.

→ CRM Lead→ CRM Deal
CRM Setup

Lost Reason

Predefined reasons for marking a lead or deal as lost (e.g. Price, Competitor, No Budget). Mandatory when closing a record as lost; feeds win/loss analysis.

→ CRM Lead→ CRM Deal
CRM Setup

CRM Product

Products or services being sold, maintained separately from ERPNext Items. Added to the products table on Leads and Deals to track what is being pitched.

→ CRM Lead (products table)→ CRM Deal (products table)
CRM Setup

SLA

Service Level Agreement defining response and resolution time targets. Assigned to Leads and Deals; uses a CRM Holiday List to exclude non-working days from SLA calculations.

→ CRM Lead→ CRM Deal← CRM Holiday List
CRM Setup

CRM Holiday List

A CRM-specific holiday calendar used exclusively by SLA calculations to skip non-working days when computing response deadlines.

→ SLA
CRM Setup

Organization

A company/account record in CRM (distinct from ERPNext Customer). Deals are linked to an Organization to group all deals under one account for account-based selling.

→ CRM Deal (organization)
CRM Setup

FCRM Settings

Global configuration for Frappe CRM — default lead owner, auto-assignment rules, email integration settings, and feature flags. Acts as the single config document for the entire CRM app.

→ CRM Lead (global config)→ CRM Deal (global config)
CRM Documents — The core pipeline records
CRM Document

CRM Lead

An unqualified sales opportunity or prospect. Contains contact details, source, industry, territory, SLA, and a products table. Can be converted into a CRM Deal. Activities (tasks, notes, calls, emails) are attached by reference.

→ CRM Deal (Convert)← Lead Status← Lead Source← Industry← Territory← Lost Reason← CRM Product← SLA← User (owner)
CRM Document

CRM Deal

A qualified sales opportunity with a value, close date, and linked organization. Tracks the full sales cycle. On winning, can sync to ERPNext as a Customer. Activities, contacts, and notes are all attached here.

→ Customer (sync on win)← Deal Status← Organization← Contact← User (owner)← SLA
CRM Activities — Actions and interactions logged against Leads/Deals
CRM Activity

CRM Task

A to-do item linked to a Lead or Deal (e.g. "Follow up call", "Send proposal"). Has a due date, assigned user, and completion status. Separate from ERPNext Project Tasks.

← CRM Lead (reference)← CRM Deal (reference)← User (assigned_to)
CRM Activity

Note

A free-text note attached to a Lead or Deal. Used for meeting summaries, internal comments, and context that doesn't fit structured fields.

← CRM Lead (reference)← CRM Deal (reference)
CRM Activity

Call Log

Records of phone calls made or received, linked to a Lead or Deal. Stores caller, duration, outcome, and recording URL if telephony integration is active.

← CRM Lead (reference)← CRM Deal (reference)
CRM Activity

Email (Communication)

Email threads linked to a Lead or Deal via Frappe's Communication doctype. Enables full email history to be visible in the CRM timeline without leaving the app.

← CRM Lead (reference)← CRM Deal (reference)
CRM Activity

Contacts

The contacts tab within a CRM Deal listing all people associated with the deal. Links to the global Contact master. Allows multiple stakeholders per deal.

← CRM Deal (contacts tab)
CRM Activity

Dashboard

The CRM analytics dashboard. Shows pipeline value, lead/deal counts by status, conversion rates, and activity summaries. Reads from CRM Lead and CRM Deal for all metrics.

→ CRM Lead (analytics)→ CRM Deal (analytics)
HR Module

Full employee lifecycle — recruitment, onboarding, leave, attendance, performance, and separation.

Recruitment — Hire-to-offer pipeline
Recruitment

Staffing Plan

Headcount budget for a department and designation over a period. Defines how many positions are approved for hiring. Raises Job Requisitions against the plan.

→ Job Requisition
Recruitment

Job Requisition

An internal request to fill a position, raised against a Staffing Plan. Contains the role, department, number of positions, and justification. Approved requisitions create Job Openings.

← Staffing Plan→ Job Opening
Recruitment

Job Opening

A published vacancy. Contains job title, description, department, and status (Open/Closed). Applicants apply against a Job Opening. Can be published to the careers portal.

← Job Requisition→ Job Applicant
Recruitment

Job Applicant

A candidate who applied for a Job Opening. Stores resume, cover letter, applicant email, and current status in the hiring pipeline. Scheduled for Interviews.

← Job Opening→ Interview
Recruitment

Interview

A scheduled interview session for a Job Applicant. Specifies round type, date/time, interviewers (Users), and expected skills. Each interviewer submits an Interview Feedback.

← Job Applicant← User (interviewer)→ Interview Feedback
Recruitment

Interview Feedback

Per-interviewer scorecard for an Interview. Contains skill ratings, overall recommendation (Proceed/Reject), and comments. Aggregated to decide on a Job Offer.

← Interview→ Job Offer
Recruitment

Job Offer

The formal offer letter document sent to a selected Job Applicant. Contains designation, salary terms, offer date, and status (Awaiting/Accepted/Rejected). On acceptance, creates an Employee record and triggers onboarding. Custom override applied in this repo.

← Interview Feedback→ Employee (creates)→ Appointment Letter→ Employee Onboarding (triggers)
Recruitment

Appointment Letter

A formal appointment/joining letter generated from an accepted Job Offer. Contains terms of employment, joining date, and can be printed on letterhead.

← Job Offer
Employee — The central HR record
Core

Employee

The master record for a staff member. Contains personal details, department, designation, date of joining, linked User account, salary structure, and emergency contacts. Almost every HR and Payroll document links back to Employee.

← Job Offer (creates)← User (user_id)← Department → Leave Policy Assignment→ Shift Assignment→ Attendance → Salary Structure Assignment→ Timesheet→ Asset (custodian) → Appraisal Cycle→ Onboarding / Transfer / Promotion / Separation
Leave — Entitlement, allocation, and application flow
Leave

Leave Type

Defines a category of leave (e.g. Annual Leave, Sick Leave, Maternity). Configures whether it is paid, encashable, carry-forward allowed, and which Holiday List applies.

→ Leave Policy (policy detail)← Holiday List
Leave

Leave Policy

A bundle of Leave Types with annual entitlement counts. Assigned to employees via Leave Policy Assignment. Defines the full leave entitlement package for a grade or role.

← Leave Type→ Leave Policy Assignment
Leave

Leave Policy Assignment

Links a Leave Policy to a specific Employee for a date range. On submission, automatically creates Leave Allocations for each Leave Type in the policy.

← Leave Policy← Employee→ Leave Allocation (auto-creates)
Leave

Leave Allocation

The actual balance granted to an employee for a specific Leave Type in a period. Can be created manually or auto-generated from a Leave Policy Assignment or Compensatory Leave Request.

← Leave Policy Assignment← Compensatory Leave Request→ Leave Application (balance used)
Leave

Leave Application

An employee's request for leave. Contains leave type, from/to dates, reason, and approver. On approval, deducts from Leave Allocation, creates a Leave Ledger Entry, marks Attendance as On Leave, and feeds LWP days into Salary Slip.

← Leave Allocation← Employee← User (approver) → Leave Ledger Entry→ Attendance→ Salary Slip (lwp days)
Leave

Leave Ledger Entry

An immutable audit log of every leave balance change (allocation, application, encashment). The source of truth for current leave balances. Auto-created; not edited manually.

← Leave Application
Leave

Leave Encashment

Converts unused leave balance into a cash payout. On submission, creates an Additional Salary entry which is then pulled into the next Salary Slip.

→ Salary Slip (via Additional Salary)
Leave

Compensatory Leave

A request to convert overtime/holiday work into compensatory leave credit. On approval, creates a Leave Allocation for the compensatory leave type.

→ Leave Allocation (creates)
Attendance & Shifts — Tracking who worked when
Attendance

Shift Type

Defines a work shift — start/end times, grace periods, auto-attendance rules, and the Holiday List. The engine that converts Employee Checkins into Attendance records automatically.

← Holiday List→ Shift Assignment→ Employee Checkin (auto-links)
Attendance

Shift Assignment

Assigns a Shift Type to an Employee for a date range. Created from Shift Requests (on approval) or directly by HR. Determines which shift rules apply when processing checkins.

← Shift Type← Employee← Shift Request
Attendance

Shift Request

An employee's request to be assigned to a specific shift. Goes through an approval workflow. On approval, creates a Shift Assignment.

→ Shift Assignment (on approval)
Attendance

Employee Checkin

Raw IN/OUT timestamps from biometric devices, mobile apps, or manual entry. The Shift Type's auto-attendance job processes these into Attendance records daily.

← Employee← Shift Type (auto-linked)→ Attendance (auto-creates)
Attendance

Attendance

Daily attendance record per employee — Present, Absent, Half Day, On Leave, Work From Home. Feeds payment_days into Salary Slip. Can be created from Checkins, Leave Applications, or Attendance Requests.

← Employee Checkin← Leave Application← Attendance Request← Employee → Salary Slip (payment_days)
Attendance

Attendance Request

An employee's request to regularize a missed or incorrect attendance entry (e.g. forgot to check in). On approval, updates or creates the Attendance record.

→ Attendance (regularizes)
Performance — Goal-setting and appraisal cycle
Performance

KRA

Key Result Area — a broad performance dimension (e.g. "Customer Satisfaction", "Revenue Growth"). Used as a template goal in Appraisal Templates to structure what employees are evaluated on.

→ Appraisal Template (template goal)
Performance

Appraisal Template

A reusable scorecard structure listing KRAs and their weightages. Used when setting up an Appraisal Cycle to define what will be measured.

← KRA→ Appraisal Cycle
Performance

Appraisal Cycle

A time-boxed performance review period (e.g. "H1 2026"). Links an Appraisal Template to a set of employees. Generates individual Appraisal documents and allows goal-setting.

← Appraisal Template← Employee→ Goal→ Appraisal
Performance

Goal

An individual SMART goal set for an employee within an Appraisal Cycle. Contains target, metric, and progress updates. Scored in the final Appraisal.

← Appraisal Cycle→ Appraisal (scored in)
Performance

Appraisal

The final performance review document for one employee in one cycle. Contains KRA scores, goal scores, self-assessment, manager feedback, and overall rating. Auto-generated by the Appraisal Cycle.

← Appraisal Cycle← Goal (scores)
Lifecycle — Post-hire employee events
Lifecycle

Employee Onboarding

A checklist-driven onboarding workflow triggered when a Job Offer is accepted. Contains tasks for IT setup, document collection, orientation, etc. Tracks completion status per activity.

← Job Offer (triggers)← Employee
Lifecycle

Employee Transfer

Records a change in an employee's department, designation, or company. Creates a history of transfers and updates the Employee master fields on submission.

← Employee
Lifecycle

Employee Promotion

Records a promotion event — change in designation, grade, or salary. Updates the Employee master and creates a history entry. Can trigger a new Salary Structure Assignment.

← Employee
Lifecycle

Employee Separation

Initiates the offboarding process when an employee resigns or is terminated. Triggers Full & Final Statement and Exit Interview. Contains a checklist of separation tasks.

← Employee→ Full & Final Statement→ Exit Interview
Lifecycle

Full & Final Statement

The settlement document for a departing employee. Calculates outstanding salary, leave encashment, gratuity, and asset returns. Links to Asset records for equipment recovery.

← Employee Separation→ Asset (asset return)
Lifecycle

Exit Interview

A structured questionnaire completed by a departing employee. Captures reasons for leaving, satisfaction scores, and feedback. Used for attrition analysis.

← Employee Separation
Expenses — Employee advance and reimbursement flow
Expenses

Employee Advance

Cash advance given to an employee before incurring expenses (e.g. for travel). The advance amount is later settled against an Expense Claim, with any balance returned or recovered.

← Employee→ Expense Claim (settled in)
Expenses

Expense Claim

An employee's reimbursement request for business expenses. Contains itemized expense lines with amounts, categories, and receipts. Can be linked to a Project/Task for cost tracking. On submission, creates a Journal Entry and optionally settles an Employee Advance.

← Employee← Employee Advance← User (approver)← Cost Center → Journal Entry (on submit)→ Project (dimension)→ Task (dimension)
Payroll Module

Salary structure definition, payroll run, and GL posting pipeline.

Payroll Setup — Define how salaries are structured
Payroll Setup

Payroll Period

Defines the tax year / payroll year boundaries. Used by Payroll Entry to determine which period a run belongs to, and by Income Tax Slab for annual tax calculations.

→ Payroll Entry (period)
Payroll Setup

Income Tax Slab

Tax bracket table defining rates at different income levels. Assigned to a Salary Structure Assignment per employee. Used during Salary Slip generation to compute TDS/income tax deductions.

← Currency→ Salary Structure Assignment
Payroll Setup

Salary Component

A single earning or deduction line item (e.g. Basic, HRA, PF, TDS). Defines whether it is an earning or deduction, the formula or amount, and the GL account to post to.

→ Salary Structure (earnings/deductions)
Payroll Setup

Salary Structure

A template that bundles Salary Components into a complete pay package. Defines the formula-based or fixed amounts for each component. Assigned to employees via Salary Structure Assignment.

← Salary Component← Currency→ Salary Structure Assignment
Payroll Setup

Salary Structure Assignment

Links a Salary Structure and Income Tax Slab to a specific Employee from a given date. The effective record used by Payroll Entry to know which structure to apply when generating Salary Slips.

← Salary Structure← Employee← Income Tax Slab→ Payroll Entry (used by)
Ad-hoc Pay — One-time additions to salary
Ad-hoc Pay

Additional Salary

A one-time earning or deduction for a specific employee in a specific month (e.g. bonus, recovery). Pulled automatically into the Salary Slip during payroll processing.

→ Salary Slip (pulled in)
Ad-hoc Pay

Employee Incentive

A performance-based incentive payment for an employee. Submitted incentives are pulled into the Salary Slip as an additional earning component.

→ Salary Slip (pulled in)
Ad-hoc Pay

Retention Bonus

A retention payment scheduled for a future date. When the payment date arrives, it is pulled into the Salary Slip as an additional earning.

→ Salary Slip (pulled in)
Payroll Run — Monthly processing and GL posting
Payroll Run

Payroll Entry

The batch payroll run document. Filters employees by Department, Cost Center, and Payroll Period, then bulk-creates Salary Slips. On submission, posts a consolidated Journal Entry for the entire payroll batch.

← Payroll Period← Salary Structure Assignment← Department (filter)← Cost Center → Salary Slip (creates, bulk)
Payroll Run

Salary Slip

The individual payslip for one employee for one month. Calculates gross pay, deductions, and net pay using the Salary Structure formulas, attendance payment days, LWP days, and any ad-hoc additions. On submission, creates a Journal Entry. Can also incorporate Timesheet costing.

← Payroll Entry← Employee← Attendance (payment_days) ← Leave Application (lwp)← Additional Salary← Employee Incentive ← Retention Bonus← Timesheet (costing)← Cost Center← Department → Journal Entry (on submit)
Projects Module

Task management, time tracking, and client billing for project-based work.

Project Setup — Configuration masters
Project Setup

Activity Type

A category of work activity (e.g. Design, Development, Testing). Used to define billing and costing rates per employee via Activity Cost. Referenced in Timesheet detail rows.

→ Activity Cost (rates per employee)
Project Setup

Activity Cost

Defines the billing rate and costing rate for a specific Employee + Activity Type combination. Fetched automatically when an employee logs time on a Timesheet.

← Activity Type← Employee→ Timesheet (fetches rates)
Project Setup

Project Template

A reusable set of tasks and milestones. When a new Project is created from a template, all template tasks are automatically created under the project, saving setup time.

→ Project (creates tasks from)
Project Documents — Core work tracking records
Project

Project

The top-level container for a piece of work. Contains status, customer, expected dates, budget, and a task list. Linked to a Customer for billing. Acts as a cost dimension on Journal Entries. Custom JS override applied in this repo.

← Project Template← Customer← User (project user) → Task→ Timesheet (parent_project)→ Journal Entry (dimension)
Project

Task

A unit of work within a Project. Has status, priority, assigned user, expected hours, and parent task (for subtask hierarchies). Time is logged against Tasks via Timesheets. Expense Claims can reference a Task. Custom JS override applied in this repo.

← Project← Task (parent_task, self-reference) → Timesheet (timesheet detail)← Expense Claim (dimension)
Project

Timesheet

A time log submitted by an employee. Contains detail rows each with Project, Task, Activity Type, hours, billing rate, and costing rate. Billable hours flow to Sales Invoice; costing can feed into Salary Slip for timesheet-based payroll.

← Employee← Project← Task← Activity Cost (rates) → Sales Invoice (billable hours)→ Salary Slip (costing)
Project

Sales Invoice

The customer-facing billing document. In the projects context, created from billable Timesheet hours. On submission, posts a Journal Entry to the accounts receivable and income accounts.

← Timesheet (billable hours)← Customer → Journal Entry (on submit)
Asset Management

Fixed asset register, depreciation, maintenance, and disposal.

Asset Setup — Configuration masters
Asset Setup

Location

A physical location where assets are kept (e.g. "Head Office - Server Room", "Warehouse A"). Used on Asset records and as the target location in Asset Movements.

→ Asset (location)← Asset Movement (target_location)
Asset Setup

Asset Category

Groups assets by type (e.g. Computers, Vehicles, Furniture). Defines the depreciation method, useful life, and GL accounts for the category. References a Finance Book for parallel depreciation.

← Finance Book→ Asset (asset_category)
Asset Setup

Maintenance Team

A named team responsible for maintaining assets. Contains team members and their roles. Assigned to Asset Maintenance records to track who is responsible for upkeep.

→ Asset Maintenance (maintenance_team)
Asset Documents — The fixed asset register and its transactions
Asset

Asset

The core fixed asset record. Contains purchase date, cost, depreciation method, useful life, custodian employee, location, and department. On submission, auto-creates a Depreciation Schedule. Custom JS override applied in this repo.

← Asset Category← Location← Item (item_code)← Finance Book ← Employee (custodian)← Department← Cost Center← Supplier (owner) → Depreciation Schedule→ Asset Movement→ Asset Repair → Asset Maintenance→ Asset Value Adjustment→ Asset Capitalization
Asset

Depreciation Schedule

Auto-generated table of monthly/yearly depreciation entries for an Asset. Each scheduled entry, when processed, creates a Journal Entry posting depreciation expense to the correct GL accounts.

← Asset (auto on submit)→ Journal Entry (monthly posting)
Asset

Asset Movement

Records the physical transfer of an Asset — between locations, employees, or departments. Updates the Asset's current location and custodian fields on submission.

← Asset→ Employee (to_employee)→ Location (target_location)
Asset

Asset Repair

Records a repair event for an Asset — repair date, cost, failure description, and repair status. If the repair cost is capitalized, creates a Journal Entry to add the cost to the asset's book value.

← Asset→ Journal Entry (if capitalized)
Asset

Asset Maintenance

A recurring maintenance schedule for an Asset. Defines maintenance tasks, frequency, and the responsible Maintenance Team. Automatically creates Maintenance Log entries as tasks come due.

← Asset← Maintenance Team→ Asset Maintenance Log
Asset

Maintenance Log

An individual maintenance task completion record. Auto-created by Asset Maintenance per scheduled task. Contains completion date, maintenance type, and notes.

← Asset Maintenance (auto per task)
Asset

Value Adjustment

Records a write-up or write-down of an Asset's book value (e.g. impairment). On submission, creates a Journal Entry to adjust the asset account and post the gain/loss.

← Asset→ Journal Entry (on submit)
Asset

Asset Capitalization

Combines multiple assets or stock items into a single capitalized Asset (e.g. assembling a server from components). The target asset is the resulting capitalized record.

← Asset (target_asset)
Accounting Spine

The financial backbone — every module ultimately posts here.

Accounting

Journal Entry

A double-entry bookkeeping document. The central posting record that every module writes to on submission — payroll, expense claims, asset depreciation, sales invoices, and more. Contains debit/credit rows referencing Accounts, Cost Centers, Departments, and Projects as dimensions.

← Salary Slip (payroll)← Expense Claim← Sales Invoice ← Asset Depreciation Schedule← Asset Repair← Asset Value Adjustment ← Account← Cost Center← Department← Project → Payment Entry (bank payment)→ GL Entry (creates)
Accounting

Payment Entry

Records an actual bank payment or receipt against a Journal Entry or invoice. Clears outstanding payables/receivables and posts the bank account movement. Used for salary bank transfers after payroll Journal Entries are created.

← Journal Entry (bank payment)
Accounting

GL Entry

The immutable general ledger row. Auto-created by Journal Entries (and other vouchers). The source of truth for all financial reports — Trial Balance, P&L, Balance Sheet. Never edited directly.

← Journal Entry (creates)
